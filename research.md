## 2. The Sober-Curious Movement

### Size & Growth
- **30% of Americans** participated in Dry January 2025 — a **36% increase** from 2024 (Circana)
- **49% of Americans** plan to drink less alcohol in 2025 — a **44% increase** from 2023
- **22% of American adults** joined Dry January 2025, up 5% from 2024
- Gen Z and Millennials are leading the charge
- Non-alcoholic beverage sales grew **132% YoY** during Dry January 2024 (Ghia)

### Cultural Shift
The sober-curious movement is redefining social norms around alcohol:
- Focus on "moderation" rather than total abstinence
- Celebrating sober experiences and gatherings
- Growing acceptance of alcohol-free lifestyles in professional settings
- Digital communities (Dry January, Hello Sunday Morning) driving awareness

## 4. Target Buyer: HR Directors

### Who Signs Off?
- **Primary:** HR Directors / VPs of People
- **Secondary:** Chief People Officer, Benefits Manager
- **Tertiary:** Executive sponsors (CEO, COO)

### Budget Cycles
- **Q1:** New fiscal year budgets finalized
- **Q2-Q3:** Implementation planning
- **Q4:** Open enrollment prep, vendor evaluation
- **Typical cycle:** 3-6 months from evaluation to contract

### Key Decision Factors
1. **ROI demonstration** — Reduced healthcare costs, absenteeism
2. **Ease of implementation** — HR admin burden is a concern
3. **Employee engagement** — Will people actually use it?
4. **Data & analytics** — Measure program effectiveness
5. **Compliance** — HIPAA, privacy considerations
